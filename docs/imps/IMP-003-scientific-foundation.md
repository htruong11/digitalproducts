# IMP-003: Scientific Foundation & Clinical Authority

## Abstract

This document provides the authoritative evidence base for the **Family Caregiver Command System (FCCS)**. To move from a "planner" to a "professional-grade transformation system," the FCCS incorporates clinical frameworks, sociological research on family dynamics, and economic methodologies for labor valuation.

By grounding our products in these scholarly foundations, we increase perceived value, build trust with high-intent buyers, and create clear differentiation from generic competitors.

## 1. The Clinical Foundation: The "Doctor Snapshot"

**Research Finding:** Standard Electronic Health Records (EHRs) are notoriously poor at capturing "geriatric syndromes." A study found that up to **90% of cases** related to falls, malnutrition, and social isolation are missed in clinical documentation (Source: NIH, 2024).

**Product Strategy (The Doctor Snapshot):**

- **Structured PGHD (Patient-Generated Health Data):** Research indicates that structured pre-consultation tools reduce clinician interpretation time by **25%** and improve "agenda setting" (Source: JMIR, 2025).
- **The 4Ms Framework:** The FCCS adopts the Institute for Healthcare Improvement (IHI) **4Ms Framework** (What Matters, Medication, Mentation, Mobility) to structure the "Doctor Snapshot." This aligns our data with the language geriatricians already use.
- **Longitudinal Baselines:** By providing home-captured gait speed, sleep patterns, and mood logs, we mitigate the "white coat effect" and memory gaps common in short consultations.

## 2. The Sociological Foundation: Sibling Equity

**Research Finding:** Caregiving is an "amplifier" of lifelong family patterns. Conflict is statistically highest between sisters. Furthermore, families that insist on strict **Equality** (50/50 splits) suffer higher burnout than those that aim for **Equity** (fairness based on ability and resources) (Source: Averyn Care / The Within-Family Differences Study, 2024).

**Product Strategy (The Sibling Equity Tracker):**

- **Expertise-Based Roles:** The FCCS encourages families to assign tasks based on "Adult Expertise" (e.g., the sibling who is a CPA handles the finances) rather than "Childhood Roles."
- **Equity Dashboard:** A visualization tool that quantifies both time and financial contributions to provide an objective "fairness" metric, reducing the subjective feeling of resentment.
- **Diffusion of Responsibility Mitigation:** In larger families, the FCCS provides a centralized "Task Board" to prevent the "I thought you were doing it" syndrome common in groups.

## 3. The Economic Foundation: Invisible Labor Valuation

**Research Finding:** Informal caregiving accounts for an estimated **2% to 4% of GDP** in developed nations but is typically excluded from economic accounting. The "Well-being Valuation" (WV) method calculates the income required to offset the decline in life satisfaction caused by caregiving (Source: Cambridge University / Eurocarers, 2024).

**Product Strategy (The Care Penalty Calculator):**

- **Valuation Methodologies:** The FCCS provides a calculator using the **Replacement Cost Method** (market rate of a home health aide) and the **Opportunity Cost Method** (lost wages).
- **The "Money Talk" Script:** Using economic valuation data to help the primary caregiver negotiate for financial support or professional help from other family members.

## 4. The Psychological Foundation: Burden & Mastery

**Research Finding:** **Self-Mastery** (the belief that one has control over life circumstances) is the strongest buffer against caregiver burnout. Furthermore, **Mindfulness-Based Interventions (MBIs)** are the "gold standard" for burden reduction (SMD -6.67) (Source: NIH Meta-Analysis, 2025).

**Product Strategy (The Mental Health Pulse):**

- **Zarit Burden Interview (ZBI-4):** The FCCS incorporates the validated 4-item brief Zarit scale as a "Weekly Pulse" to identify high-risk burnout before it happens.
- **Mastery-Based Training:** Our Micro-Courses focus on **Medical Task Mastery** (teaching the 55% of caregivers who do complex nursing tasks how to do them safely), which directly increases the "Self-Mastery" buffer.
