# IMP-005: Caregiver Digital Product Market Research & Direction

## Abstract

People are making money on caregiver-adjacent digital products, but the market is
price-compressed and crowded. The winning move is not another generic
"caregiver planner." The first product should be a sharper **family handoff and
aging-parent coordination kit** that solves one stressed buyer moment:

> "I need my family, aides, and doctors to have the right information without me
> explaining everything from scratch."

Keep the Etsy MVP from IMP-001/IMP-004, but reposition it away from broad binder
language and toward handoff, emergency readiness, doctor visit prep, and family
task coordination.

## Scope

In scope:

- Check whether similar products have visible demand.
- Identify competitor clusters and price ranges.
- Decide whether the current Etsy PDF MVP should continue.
- Recommend product direction, pricing, and listing angles.

Out of scope:

- Paid keyword tools such as eRank, EverBee, Marmalead, or Alura.
- Exact revenue estimates. Etsy exposes shop sales and review counts unevenly;
  marketplace review counts are useful demand signals, not exact unit sales.
- Medical/legal advice claims or any HIPAA/compliance promise.

## Findings

1. **The market exists.** Etsy has live market pages for caregiver planner,
   caregiving log, elder care planner, aging parent planner, and medical binder
   products. The "caregiver planner" market page shows 5,000+ items; "medical
   binder" shows 4,000+ items; "aging parents planner" shows 352+ items.

2. **There is visible seller traction.** CompetentCaregiver is a direct
   caregiver/senior-care Etsy shop with 5,793 sales, 537 reviews, 43 active
   items, and 4 years on Etsy. Its current products are mostly cheap digital
   downloads: senior activity packs around $0.59 and health/caregiver toolkits
   around $2.99 sale price.

3. **Generic planner pricing is low.** Many caregiver log / planner / binder
   listings sit between $0.99 and $8.00, with occasional larger bundles around
   $12-$15 and a few premium products near $20-$30.

4. **The $17 IMP price is possible but not automatic.** A $17 product must look
   meaningfully more useful than a commodity printable. It needs stronger
   positioning, better product structure, and clearer listing images than the
   $1-$5 listings.

5. **The broad "medical binder" keyword is validated but brutal.** Etsy's
   medical-binder pages show huge review counts and many under-$10 digital
   products. Competing there first risks being buried under broad health,
   chronic illness, family medical history, and emergency binder products.

6. **The narrow caregiving-log keyword is also validated but commoditized.**
   Caregiving log pages show many daily checklist, timesheet, task sheet, and
   home-health report products. These sell, but the product surface is easy to
   copy and price competition is severe.

7. **Aging-parent coordination is the cleaner wedge.** "Aging parent planner"
   and "elder care planner" have fewer visible items than generic caregiver
   planner and medical binder pages, while still showing buyer intent around
   emergency info, medication tracking, appointments, and family organization.

8. **Premium competitors are moving into connected systems.** Notion caregiver
   templates list around $20-$30 and promise connected databases, family
   updates, document vaults, and medication/appointment systems. This validates
   the IMP-002 ladder from Etsy PDF wedge to spreadsheet/Notion-style operating
   system.

9. **App competitors validate the bigger problem but are not the MVP path.**
   CareHaven positions around caregiver mental load, handoffs, doctor visits,
   privacy, and multi-caregiver sync, with paid tiers at $7.99/mo and
   $15.99/mo. That is proof of a larger software-shaped problem, but building an
   app now would violate the lean MVP strategy.

10. **Etsy policy supports original digital downloads, with constraints.** Etsy
    permits sellers' original digital designs and seller-prompted AI creations,
    but AI use must be disclosed in the listing description. Etsy can remove
    listings that resell pre-existing designs or manufacturer templates.

11. **The business remains low-cost to test.** Etsy charges a $0.20 listing fee
    and 6.5% transaction fee; Offsite Ads can add 12%-15% on attributed orders.
    The unit economics for digital files remain fine. The real risks are traffic,
    conversion, differentiation, and buyer trust.

## Decision

Continue the Etsy digital-download MVP, but change the launch angle.

Do **not** launch as:

- "Family Caregiver Command Kit"
- "Caregiver Planner"
- "Medical Binder"
- "Elder Care Binder"

Those are acceptable secondary keywords, but they are too generic as the main
promise.

Launch as one of these sharper angles:

1. **Aging Parent Handoff Kit**
2. **Family Caregiver Handoff Binder**
3. **Aging Parent Emergency & Care Handoff Kit**

Recommended first listing title direction:

> Aging Parent Handoff Kit - Caregiver Binder, Emergency Info, Medication
> Tracker, Doctor Visit Notes, Family Task Planner, Printable PDF

Primary promise:

> One printable kit so family members, aides, and doctors can quickly understand
> what changed, what matters, and who is handling what.

## Product Direction

Build one **10-14 page PDF** that is calmer and more operational than the cheap
printables.

Required pages:

1. Start Here: fill these 3 pages first.
2. One-page emergency snapshot.
3. Medication and refill tracker.
4. Doctor visit prep sheet.
5. Doctor visit notes and follow-up actions.
6. Daily handoff log.
7. Weekly care summary.
8. Family task ownership sheet.
9. Key contacts directory.
10. Important documents inventory.
11. "What changed this week?" review page.
12. Printing and sharing instructions.
13. Disclaimer: organizer only, not medical/legal/financial advice.

Optional bonus:

- A one-page "care handoff text message template" buyers can copy into a family
  group chat.
- Later, a Google Sheets / Notion / Airtable version if the PDF gets sales or
  messages asking for editability.

## Pricing Direction

Test price, do not debate it for weeks.

Recommended launch prices:

- First listing: **$9.99 intro price** for the first 30 days or first 10 sales.
- Anchor price shown in copy/mockups: **$14.99**.
- Raise to **$14.99-$17.00** only after sales, favorites, or messages prove
  value.

Reasoning:

- $17 is defendable for a polished coordination kit, but many visible comps are
  $1-$8.
- Starting at $9.99 lowers conversion friction while still avoiding the
  race-to-the-bottom $0.99 tier.
- A later $27 product should be a connected system, not the first PDF.

## Listing Direction

Lead with stressed-caregiver outcomes, not page count.

Use buyer language:

- aging parent
- family caregiver
- care handoff
- emergency info
- medication tracker
- doctor visit notes
- family task planner
- elder care organizer
- printable PDF
- caregiver binder

Avoid:

- "AI-generated"
- "clinical"
- "HIPAA compliant"
- "medical advice"
- "legal organizer"
- "prevents mistakes"
- "improves health outcomes"

Listing images should show:

- One-page emergency snapshot.
- Daily handoff log filled with realistic sample data.
- Family task ownership page.
- Doctor visit prep page.
- Full kit contents image.
- Clear "printable PDF, US Letter + A4" badge.
- Plain disclaimer in the final listing image or description.

## Why This Direction

- It keeps the low-cost Etsy MVP intact.
- It avoids competing head-on with massive generic medical-binder listings.
- It uses the strongest real buyer pain: handoff and coordination.
- It creates a natural upgrade path to Google Sheets, Notion, and eventually a
  lightweight app or B2B facility product.
- It is safer from a claims perspective because it sells organization, not care
  guidance.
