# IMP-001: Lean Caregiver Digital Product MVP

## Publication Safety Note

IMP-006 supersedes this document for the first publishable product. Treat
"Family Caregiver Command Kit" as historical working language. The current
publication path is the Hospital-to-Home Discharge Kit, framed only as an
organizer that records care-team instructions and family tasks.

## Abstract

Build the fastest credible path to first revenue from the source session:
a low-touch digital product business that can be run by one person with agent
help.

The decision is to launch one family caregiver coordination product on Etsy.
The product is files, not software: a print-ready PDF kit, plus an editable
Google Sheet only if it is quick to produce. The goal is first sales and real
buyer signal, not a polished brand system.

Cut everything that delays a paid listing: SaaS, websites, domains, Shopify,
Gumroad, Pinterest operations, paid research tools, Canva dependencies, custom
apps, checkout code, subscriptions, and broad product lines.

## Scope

In scope:

- Re-read the source session and preserve the actual business constraints.
- Choose the leanest product, channel, and validation path.
- Rank the next tasks by what most directly gets to revenue.
- Separate agent work from human-only account and approval work.

Out of scope for MVP:

- SaaS, login systems, Stripe, backend code, custom apps, or hosted tools.
- Medical, legal, tax, financial, or caregiving advice.
- Pinterest, Gumroad, a standalone website, domain work, Shopify, Tailwind,
  ads, or multiple product niches before the first Etsy sale.
- A 50-page planner, content brand, newsletter, or course.

## Findings

1. The source session's core constraint is passive or semi-passive income for a
   1-2 person operation. That rules out trades SaaS, services, agency work, and
   anything requiring sales calls, onboarding, implementation, or high support.

2. The caregiver niche remains the best first bet from the session. It combines
   a large audience, emotional urgency, clear pain, and a product gap around
   family coordination: handoffs, medications, appointments, tasks, contacts,
   and important document inventory.

3. The strongest MVP is not "an app" or "a website." It is a self-serve Etsy
   digital download. A PDF opens everywhere, prints cleanly, and reduces support
   compared with editable template workflows.

4. The editable Google Sheet is useful, but it should be a bonus. Making it the
   main promise adds Google copy/share friction for an audience that may prefer
   print.

5. Pinterest is valid long-term distribution but is not the launch engine. It
   should start after the first sale or after the Etsy listing proves that the
   offer is worth compounding.

6. Paid keyword tools such as eRank or EverBee are helpful, but they are not a
   prerequisite for the first build decision. The immediate need is faster:
   inspect live Etsy results and review language, then build the smallest
   differentiated kit.

7. The previous drafts over-expanded the work by including Pinterest, multiple
   listings, paid research tools, broad validation tables, and a larger product
   spec. The MVP should be one listing, one kit, one success gate.

8. The source session includes web-search summaries, but the underlying research
   artifacts are not preserved in this repo. Treat those as directional, not as
   hard evidence. Re-check live marketplace evidence before publishing.

## Decision

Launch working title: **Family Caregiver Command Kit**.

Positioning:

- Primary wedge: "coordinate care between family members."
- Secondary benefits: fewer missed tasks, cleaner handoffs, better appointment
  notes, easier emergency information sharing.
- Avoid: broad "elderly care advice," clinical claims, diagnosis language,
  legal/financial guidance, or promises of better health outcomes.

Initial channel:

- Etsy only.

Initial deliverable:

- Print-ready PDF kit, roughly 10-14 pages.
- Optional Google Sheet bonus if it adds less than one hour.
- Free sample only if needed for a smoke test or listing preview.

Initial price:

- Start in the low-ticket Etsy range, likely $12-$17.
- Raise only after sales, reviews, or buyer questions prove value.

Success gate:

- Review after 10 sales or 100 qualified Etsy listing visits, whichever comes
  first.
- If there are no sales after 100 qualified visits, fix offer, title, images,
  or price before building another product.
- If sales happen but support questions repeat, fix instructions before adding
  channels or products.

## Simplest Solutions

Do first:

- Validate with live Etsy search and competitor reviews for one focused angle.
- Build the PDF kit in plain language.
- Create clean listing images from the actual pages.
- Publish one Etsy listing.
- Measure views, favorites, messages, conversion, and support friction.

Defer until triggered:

- Pinterest: after first sale or clear Etsy conversion signal.
- eRank/EverBee: if live Etsy browsing cannot answer demand/competition
  questions.
- Gumroad: if Etsy rejects the product or off-platform traffic appears.
- Website/domain/Shopify: after repeatable sales.
- Airbnb host kit: after the caregiver listing reaches the success gate.

Refuse for MVP:

- SaaS, apps, subscriptions, Stripe, databases, custom checkout, or account
  systems.
- Canva Pro assets or template resale complexity.
- Medical, legal, financial, tax, or caregiving advice.
- A large planner whose polish delays the first listing.

## MVP Product Spec

Paid PDF kit, approximately 10-14 pages:

1. Start Here: 5-minute setup and what to fill out first.
2. Emergency information sheet.
3. Medication and refill tracker.
4. Appointment tracker.
5. Doctor visit notes.
6. Daily care handoff log.
7. Family task / who-does-what tracker.
8. Key contacts directory.
9. Important document inventory checklist.
10. Weekly care review page.
11. Simple printing/use instructions.
12. Disclaimer: organizer only, not medical, legal, or financial advice.

Optional bonus:

- One editable Google Sheet with the same core trackers.

Free sample, only if useful:

- Emergency information sheet.
- One daily handoff log.

## Ranked Next Tasks

1. **Live Etsy competitor pass.** Search the exact buyer angles below and record
   the first-page listings, prices, review counts, format, page count, image
   promises, and review complaints. This is the highest-leverage task because it
   determines positioning before any asset work.

2. **Pick one listing angle.** Choose the search phrase with enough demand and a
   visible gap. Do not build for four angles at once.

3. **Write the kit content.** Draft only the 10-14 MVP pages in neutral
   organizer language. Do not expand into advice or a full caregiver guide.

4. **Lay out and render the PDF.** Use simple print-friendly HTML/CSS or another
   local file workflow. Verify the PDF prints cleanly and is readable in black
   and white.

5. **Create listing images from the real product.** Use page screenshots,
   close-ups, and a clear contents image. Avoid fake lifestyle filler.

6. **Write one Etsy listing.** Title, tags, description, FAQ, post-purchase note,
   and support replies should match the chosen angle.

7. **Human creates or confirms Etsy seller setup.** Identity, payout, tax, 2FA,
   and publish approval are human-only.

8. **Publish and measure to the gate.** Track views, favorites, messages, sales,
   and buyer questions until 10 sales or 100 qualified listing visits.

## Agent vs Human Work

Agent can do:

- Live marketplace/review research.
- Product copy and page structure.
- PDF layout and render checks.
- Optional Google Sheet structure.
- Listing image drafts.
- Etsy title, tags, description, FAQ, and support snippets.
- Post-launch measurement summaries and listing revision drafts.

Human must do:

- Own Etsy seller account, identity, payout, tax, and 2FA.
- Approve public product claims and listing copy.
- Click publish.
- Handle refunds, account holds, legal/tax terms, and customer messages that
  require judgment.

## Risks and Controls

- **Etsy cold start:** start with one sharp listing and use real buyer language
  from competitor reviews. Do not dilute effort across channels.
- **Free-printable competition:** sell the complete coordination system, not
  loose forms.
- **Support burden:** PDF-first, Start Here page, simple printing instructions,
  and canned replies.
- **Liability:** neutral organizer language and a visible disclaimer.
- **Weak validation:** rerun live Etsy checks before publishing; do not rely only
  on the transcript's summarized research.
- **Scope creep:** no Pinterest, website, Gumroad, or second product until the
  success gate.
